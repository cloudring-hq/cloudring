A mere 10% of the potential market has adopted cloud services
When discussing cloud services, it is important to recognize their diverse nature rather than treating
them as a single, uniform entity. AWS, the leading cloud provider, is utilized by 1 million companies
(or more accurately, users) out of a total of 333 million companies . In general, it can be estimated
that merely around 3 million companies employ cloud services , with the majority of these
organizations having an IT infrastructure in place.

Unresolved issues of̶ c̶l:ı̶e̶n̶t̶s̶ partners
Cloud technologies have introduced a new way of building IT systems. The cloud has made it convenient to connect to shared pools of configurable computing
resources and manage them simply and independently.
Drawbacks of modern clouds:
• Dependence on a single provider in a single jurisdiction (vendor lock-in)
Lack of trust in data processing by a third-party provider in a specific (or any) jurisdiction.
Reliance on human decisions concerning business and regulations.
Decision-making not always in the best interest of the client
• Lack of control over your IT (trust, but verify)
These are no longer your systems, even if installed in your data center.
Absence of a focus on verifiable algorithmic security and provable transparency.
• While a single cloud technology provider, as the center of a client's IT, strives to solve all IT problems,
it cannot One company cannot be an expert in all areas (Gazprom Neft Bazen platform, Medsi image analysis service, Self Driving Cloud platforms, Web3 technologies).
The development of a cloud vendor does not fully realize the potential of partners (all clients are partners).
The development of First Class Citizen services is only available to the vendor, while other developers are second-class citizens (imagine an OS where you cannot install applications – the out-of-the-box offering is sufficient for everyone) – the App
Service Store model is ignored, as it was ignored by Microsoft in the PC market for decades (but there was the possibility of installing third-party applications in a non-standard way). The cloud is an operating platform for data centers, enabling the
launch and sale of next-generation multi-user applications - services.
Technologies being developed are typically not available to other providers (especially in markets or audiences where there is no interest or opportunity for development now) – thousands of companies are developing the same things.
Diversification should occur through added value – new service solutions and new ways of use.
Cloud technologies are not fully available in private data centers, including for a fee; large companies develop their own internal clouds (Tinkoff, Avito, MTS, Yandex, etc.) instead of developing solutions specifically tailored for them.
The capabilities of hybrid clouds are limited between providers, providers and private clouds, private clouds and partners (peer federations), and developers who create a fundamentally new channel for delivering cloud solutions.
The local development of cloud services is complicated, and clients not yet using the cloud are not covered (cloud technologies can be used ubiquitously).
Instead of addressing the longstanding issues, cloud companies are "dealing with objecNons" out of concern for their business.

Monetization
• All existing scenarios for cloud providers and various types of partner channels.
• Private clouds for large companies with Enterprise extensions (managing their infrastructure for products from a business standpoint to maximize
infrastructure utilization). Private Cloud Extensions for integration into corporate systems and adding steps to processes. A necessary set of services from
the public cloud within private infrastructure. Payment for licenses (per service, per enterprise component), for private PCE customizations, for support and
maintenance (ensuring functionality in the area of responsibility, updates). Upon license expiration, functionality is preserved, but maintenance and updates
for enterprise components and products (except for critical fixes, essential for ensuring future compatibility) are discontinued. The ability to connect to
central clouds and purchase some services from there (which do not make sense in private) and the possibility of joint use.
• Other providers: the opportunity to build a full-fledged provider in any jurisdiction in an equal federation or without it, with payment based on a Revenue
Sharing model + cost of deployment and adaptation to business specifics + cost of prioritizing customizations when necessary + cost of maintenance and
support. Upon license expiration, functionality is preserved, but maintenance and updates for enterprise components and products (except for critical fixes,
essential for ensuring future compatibility) are discontinued. The Revenue Sharing percentage decreases for in-house developed products and enterprise
components if they are supplied on the platform with full installation or through the federated channel (depending on the volume of sales on the platform,
it may drop for partner services to 0 if the partner service is profitable when sold through the main channel).
• EDGE - #CloudRING OpenSource + PCE when needed + a necessary set of services. Payment for support, maintenance, and updates. Upon license expiration,
functionality is preserved, but maintenance and updates for products are discontinued. Various models: Connected (using the Business Component and
Edge service in the provider and other provider services) and Disconnected without connecting to the center.
• Dev/Small: 1. Independent developers and ISVs can download and automatically deploy #CloudRING OpenSource with Compute, Network, Compute, and
services where SSPL is required. Use it for service development on their own. Receive platform updates. Developer support is free, with prioritization
available at an additional cost.
• For customers who are not yet ready to pay for the cloud and transition: Use developed and basic services in-house (competing with ESXI, HyperV, KVM,
OpenStack – it's better to use ours), license paid services through the Service Store, connect to the central cloud and use services from there when
necessary, together. Paid support is available.
